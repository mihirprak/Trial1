Group strategic report
For the year ended 31 March 2025

Introduction
The directors present the strategic report for the year ended 31 March 2025.

Principal activity and strategy
Fibrus deploys full fibre broadband infrastructure to homes and businesses across Northern Ireland and parts of North England. Strategy focuses on regional and rural areas underserved by legacy networks, and uses government funding (in NI and Cumbria) where commercially required. Focus is on first-to-market entry, value, and service.

Operating review
Fibrus has become a leading independent full fibre operator in the UK & Ireland, with continued delivery on build and customer take-up.

Build programme delivery
March 2025: Delivered second milestone of BDUK Gigabit project in Cumbria — full-fibre to ~12,500 homes/businesses (part of initial £108m contract for 32,000 premises).
March 2025: Contract extended; now additional 21,000 premises in Cumbria; total subsidy increased to £150m.
June 2025: Completed "Stratum" programme — full fibre to 81,148 homes across Northern Ireland.
Economic impact (since 2020, per independent report):

£431 million total economic value generated
£74 million from company operations (turnover, wages, jobs)
£271 million in supply chain investment
£86 million indirect local spending by employees
435 people employed by Fibrus
£50.6 million potential annual productivity gains
Over 411K charitable contributions across NI (note: original had a formatting issue)
Northern Ireland connectivity now ~94%.

Rapid customer take-up
Connected customers grew by ~35k (45% growth) — penetration 27.9% of ready-for-service homes.
Annualised churn: 5.4% (over half uncontrollable such as home moves or bad debt).
Key metrics (Mar 22 → Mar 25)

Connected customers (000s): 9.5 → 113.5
Premises RFS (000s): 113.4 → 407.3
Penetration: 8.4% → 27.9%
Principal risks and uncertainties
Key risks: competition, customer take-up, churn, tech change, economic risk (interest/inflation), credit risk, liquidity risk, interest rate risk (mitigated via swaps).
